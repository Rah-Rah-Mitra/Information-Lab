# Computer Vision Bibliography and Research Landscape

*This document provides a comprehensive list of references spanning several decades of computer vision research. It covers fundamental topics such as 3D reconstruction, motion estimation, and image processing. The list serves as a scholarly index for researchers in the field.*

This note provides a high-level overview of the research landscape within [[Computer Vision]], as evidenced by the extensive bibliography provided in the source text. The field is characterized by a diverse array of sub-disciplines and complex mathematical and computational challenges.

## Concept
[[Computer Vision]] is the scientific field dedicated to understanding and interpreting visual information from digital images and videos. The provided references highlight several core pillars of the research:

- **3D Reconstruction**: The process of recovering  ability to estimate 3D structures from 2D images. This includes techniques like [[Structure from Motion]] and [[Neural Radiance Fields]].
- **Motion Estimation**: The estimation of movement between frames, often involving [[Optical Flow]] and [[Stereo Matching]].
- **Image Processing**: The fundamental manipulation of digital signals to enhance, restore, or transform images. This includes [[Image Segmentation]], [[Image Processing]], and texture synthesis.
- **Object and Face Recognition**: The identification and the categorization of entities within a scene, which includes [[Object Detection]], [[Object Recognition]], and [[Face Recognition]].
- **Geometric and Photometric Analysis**: The study of geometric properties and light-based information, such as [[Stereo Matching]] and depth estimation.
- **Neural and Deep Learning Approaches**: Modern research heavily utilizes [[Neural Radiance Fields]] and deep learning architectures to solve classical vision tasks.
