# The Arnoldi Iteration And Krylov Subspaces

*The Arnoldi iteration is a Gram-Schmidt-style process used to construct orthonormal bases for Krylov subspaces and reduce nonhermitian matrices to Hessenberg form. It provides optimal polynomial approximations to the matrix-vector product, and its Ritz values serve as as approximations to the extreme eigenvalues of a matrix. This method is particularly useful for large-scale problems where direct methods are computationally prohibitive.*

The [[Arnoldi Iteration]] is a fundamental iterative method used to construct orthonormal bases for successive [[Krylov Subspaces]] and to reduce a [[Nonhermitian Matrix]] to [[Hessenberg Form]].

## Concept
Given a matrix $A$ and an initial vector $b$, the [[Krylov Subspace]] $\mathcal{K}_n$ is defined as the span of the vectors $b, Ab, A^2b, \dots, A^{n-1}b$.

$$ \mathcal{K}_n = \text{span} \{ b, Ab, A^2b, \dots, A^{n-1}b \} $$

This vector sequence forms the basis of the [[Arnoldi Iteration]], which uses a modified [[Gram-Schmidt Orthogonalization]] to transform the matrix $A$ into a Hessenberg matrix $H_n$ through an orthogonal similarity transformation $A Q_n = Q_n H_n$. The resulting matrix $H_n$ is an $(n+1) \times n$ upper-left section of a larger reduction, and its eigenvalues are known as [[Ritz Values]].

## Polynomial Approximation
One of the most powerful interpretations of the [[Arnoldi Iteration]] is its connection to [[Polynomial Approximation]]. A vector $x$ in the Krylov subspace $\mathcal{K}_n$ can be expressed as $x = q(A)b$ for some polynomial $q(z)$ of degree at most $n-1$. The iteration solves the following minimization problem:

$$ \min_{p_n \in \mathcal{P}_n} \| p_n(A)b \| $$

where $\mathcal{P}_n$ is the space of monic polynomials of degree $n$. The solution to this problem is the unique monic polynomial $p_n$ which is the characteristic polynomial of the Hessenberg matrix $H_n$. Consequently, the [[Ritz Values]] generated by the iteration are the roots of this optimal polynomial.

## Convergence and Eigenvalues
In practice, the [[Arnoldi Iteration]] is used to find the extreme [[Eigenvalue]]s of a [[Nonhermitian Matrix]]. While the method is can be highly sensitive to the nonnormality of the matrix (where eigenvectors are far from orthogonal), the [[Ritz Values]] typically converge to the eigenvalues with the largest modulus or those near the edge of the spectrum.
