# Geospatial Artificial Intelligence Research Bibliography

*A collection of academic references focusing on foundation models, representation learning, and location encoding within the field of GeoAI.*

[[Geospatial Artificial Intelligence]] (GeoAI) is an interdisciplinary field that integrates artificial intelligence techniques to analyze geospatial data. The provided bibliography highlights several key research directions in this domain, including the development of [[Foundation Models]] for earth monitoring and urban general intelligence, as well as advancements in [[Representation Learning]] for spatial features and polygonal geometries.

## Research Focus Areas

### Location Encoding
[[Location Encoding]] is a critical component of GeoAI, with research focusing on methods for representing coordinates and spatial relationships on spherical surfaces or through grid cells to improve the prediction accuracy of geospatial models.

### Foundation Models
Recent literature emphasizes the transition toward general-purpose foundation models that can be transferred across different regions and tasks, moving beyond task-specific models to achieve broader geospatial intelligence.
