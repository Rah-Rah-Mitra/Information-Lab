# Economic Information in Integer Programming

*Integer programming models differ from linear programming in how economic information, such as shadow prices, shadow prices, and reduced costs, can be extracted. Unlike LP, IP models often lack clear marginal valuations for constraints, and the presence of duality gaps and duality gaps can make traditional shadow price interpretation difficult. Reformulation through the convex hull can instead provide meaningful economic information.*

This note explores the extraction of economic information from [[Integer Programming]] models, highlighting the differences between [[Linear Programming]] models. 

## Concept
In [[Linear Programming]], [[Shadow Prices]] are well-defined and provide a marginal valuation for constraints. However, in [[Integer Programming]], the relationship between the duality relationship fails. The difference between the maximum objective of the primal IP problem and the maximum objective of its LP relaxation is known as the [[Duality Gap]]. 

$$ \text{max } P \text{ (IP)} < \text{max } P \text{ (LP)} $$ 

This formula models the objective value of the IP problem relative to its LP relaxation. 

In [[Integer Programming]], [[Shadow Prices]] can be ambiguous or non-theoretically sound. For example, a constraint with positive slack in an LP model represents a a free good, and has a zero shadow price. In an IP model, a constraint with positive slack may still have a positive economic value. 

One way to recover meaningful economic information is to recover the [[Convex Hull]] of the feasible integer solutions. If the model is reformulated as the [[Convex Hull]], the model can be treated as an LP problem and shadow prices can be obtained with desirable properties.
